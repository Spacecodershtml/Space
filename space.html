History Of Space

The history of space goes back to ancient times when people initially looked at the stars curiously, and as time passed by more new technology and space inventions began happening. The 20th century signified a shift with the introduction of space exploration, creating the launch of the initial satellite Sputnik 1, by the Soviet Union in 1957. This was succeeded by major milestones, such as Yuri Gagarin's groundbreaking flight in 1961 as the first person in space. NASA's Apollo 11 crew's Moon landing in 1969 showcased humanity's ability to travel to and investigate far-off celestial objects, motivating years of additional exploration and technological progress.

Facts About Space

  1. The universe is expanding. Observations show that galaxies are moving away from us, indicating that the space between them is stretching.
  2. One million Earths could fit inside the Sun, making it massively larger than our home planet.
  3. There is water in space. Water vapor has been found around some stars, and ice can be found on the moon and other celestial bodies.
  4. A day on Venus is longer than a year on Earth due to its slow rotation, taking 243 Earth days to complete one rotation but only 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun.
  5. The largest known star is UY Scuti, which is around 1,700 times larger than the Sun.
